    • Lil Skex, an artist.

    Lil Skex, born Olamide Mihlali Blessing Arowosegbe, embodies Johannesburg’s gritty energy through his South African Xhosa and Nigerian Yoruba roots. His music captures the struggle for discipline, personal loss, and the harsh realities of urban life. With influences from jazz, hip hop, soul, and philosophical musings, Skex dives into themes of free will, spiritual knowledge, and the cracks in our democracy.

    His lyrics cut through the contradictions of chasing wealth while navigating systemic traps, survival in tough neighbourhoods, and the search for real connections. He reflects on his own attitude, moral compass, high ambitions, and the global struggles of malnourished countries. His words confront the violence and inequality faced by oppressed communities, expressing solidarity with Palestine and critiquing the systemic suppression of Black culture.

    Blending the chaos of Lagos, the depth of the Eastern Cape, and Jozi’s raw energy, Skex’s music offers a raw and introspective look at the human experience. His art captures the grimy and complexity of modern life, revealing the challenges and contradictions of navigating the world.
